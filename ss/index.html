Project Objectives

This project addresses a critical gap in UK rail intelligence: the need to transform raw operational data into actionable insights. Leveraging a dataset covering 19,871 distinct trips and 31.7K ticket transactions across 65 unique routes.

01

Optimize Rail Operations

Identify inefficiencies in scheduling, staffing, and resource allocation by analyzing historical journey data and delay patterns.

02

Understand Passenger Behavior

Map passenger demand across peak and off-peak windows, revealing patterns that can guide dynamic pricing and capacity planning.

03

Evaluate Revenue Streams

Compare the financial performance of Advance, Off-Peak, and Anytime ticket classes to uncover pricing optimization opportunities.

04

Improve On-Time Performance

Diagnose the root causes of delays and cancellations, quantify their operational and financial impact, and propose targeted interventions.

Key Pre-Processing Challenges Overcome:

  • 🛠️ Fixing Delayed Status Errors We found 18 trips marked as 'Delayed' or 'Cancelled' even though their actual arrival time was exactly on time; we fixed these errors using a SQL query to set their status back to 'On Time'.
  • ✍️ Standardizing Messy Text Fields The delay reason columns were inconsistent, using different terms for the same thing (like 'Signal failure' vs 'Signal Failure'). We unified all text categories to group correctly in dashboard charts.
  • 💸 Calculating Hidden Refunds & Profit Raw data only showed ticket prices without accounting for losses from disruptions. We created a dynamic calculation rule based on UK rail logic to estimate refunds (from 5% to 100% based on delay minutes) to find the true net profit.
  • 🌙 Handling Midnight Trips (Overnight Durations) For trains running around midnight, subtracting departure time from arrival time created broken negative numbers (like -1400 minutes). We solved this by adding a condition (+1440 minutes) to fix the journey duration.

Business Recommendations for UK National Rail

Actionable, data-driven strategies designed to optimize capacity and protect net profits

1

Implement Dynamic Pricing for Off-Peak Windows

Since Off-Peak journeys dominate volume at 77.39%, introducing targeted promo fares during quiet midday and late-evening hours will help redistribute commuter demand, maximize train utilization, and drive incremental revenue.

2

Target Infrastructure Investment on Major Delay Causes

With Weather (26.34%), Technical Issues (25.00%), and Signal Failures (20.71%) driving over 72% of all delays, Network Rail must prioritize maintenance budgets specifically on the routes and equipment most vulnerable to these three factors.

3

Enhance Real-Time Delay & Refund Communication

Since train delays average 38 minutes and lead to £40K in refund payouts, integrating real-time delay tracking and automated, instant refund claims into passenger apps will improve satisfaction and streamline the process.

4

Establish a Unified Rail Data Platform

Building on our data cleaning workflow that successfully harmonized 19.9K records, creating a unified database that connects journey logs, ticketing, and weather data will accelerate future analytics and support proactive decision-making.
